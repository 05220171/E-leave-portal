Our Team

Rinchen Dem & Karma Deejay Tshomo


The JNEC E-Leave Portal was developed in 2025 by two students of Jigme Namgyel Engineering college, pursuing their diploma in computer system and network in final year. They are passionate about crafting student-focused digital platforms that streamline processes and create positive experiences.

Driven by a desire to apply their technical skills to solve a real-world challenge within their own college community, they undertook the development of this E-Leave system. Their vision was to create a centralized, accessible platform that reduces administrative overhead and provides a clear, reliable way for students and staffs to manage their leave requirements.Rinchen and Karma hope this website proves valuable to the JNEC community.

Guidance and Mentorship:

This project was developed under the invaluable guidance and mentorship of Mr. Yonten Tshering and Mr. Rinchen Tobgay, esteemed lecturers at Jigme Namgyel Engineering College. Their expertise in software development, project management, and keen understanding of the college's operational needs were instrumental throughout the entire development lifecycle.

Mr. Younten Tshering played a key role in overall shaping the project's scope and features, aligning them with the practical requirements of an effective e-leave system. His encouragement and problem-solving support were crucial in overcoming technical challenges and keeping the project on track.

Mr. Rinchen Tobgay provided critical insights into system architecture and database design, ensuring the portal's robustness and scalability. His continuous feedback on user interface and experience helped refine the platform to be intuitive and user-friendly for both students and staff.

The development team is profoundly grateful for their dedication, patience, and the countless hours they invested in guiding this project to fruition. Their mentorship not only contributed to the success of the JNEC E-Leave Portal but also provided an enriching learning experience for the student developers.
